BitAxe Bitcoin Miner

Solo Mining Bitcoin & Learning the Hard Way

Project Overview

Running a BitAxe Gamma solo Bitcoin miner as both an educational project and a tiny lottery ticket. While the odds of finding a block are astronomical, it's a hands-on way to learn about Bitcoin mining, proof-of-work, and network difficulty.

The BitAxe contributes to the Bitcoin network, providing real-world experience with mining operations. It's fascinating to watch it contribute hashes to the global network in real-time.

Hardware Specifications

  • Model: BitAxe Gamma
  • Hashrate: 978 GH/s (Average: 981 GH/s)
  • Expected Hashrate: 1.12 TH/s
  • Power Consumption: 20W
  • Input Voltage: 5.1V (5V nominal)
  • Efficiency: 20.45 J/TH (Average: 20.42 J/TH)
  • ASIC Frequency: 550 MHz
  • ASIC Voltage: 1.15V (measured)
  • ASIC Temperature: 59.9°C
  • VR Temperature: 69°C
  • Fan Speed: 38% (4,358 RPM)

Mining Statistics

  • Pool: public-pool.io (Port 21496)
  • Total Shares: 17,562
  • Job Not Found: 21 (0.12%)
  • Difficulty Too Low: 6 (0.03%)
  • Best Share (All-Time): 27.7G
  • Best Share (Current Boot): 354M
  • Uptime: 3 weeks, 4 days, 11 hours, 20 minutes

Why Run a BitAxe?

Running a BitAxe is about contributing to the Bitcoin network's security and decentralization. Every hash helps secure the blockchain and strengthens the overall network.

It's also an educational tool—hands-on experience with proof-of-work mining provides real understanding of how Bitcoin actually works, beyond just theory.

Plus, there's the lottery aspect: while the odds are extremely low, solo mining means there's always a chance of finding a block.
